Why I love trying new foods

I love exploring new foods and cuisines because I believe food is one of the best ways to experience a culture. Whenever I travel, trying local dishes is what I look forward to the most. Someday, I’d love to travel across Asia and taste the diversity of regional foods. Some things on my bucket list are trying street food in Korea, dining at a hawker center in Singapore, or wandering through a busy night market in Thailand.

FAQ

Question Answer
What are some cuisines you recommend? I love the flavors of Vietnamese, Korean, and Thai food and I highly recommend trying the variety of noodle dishes, both stir fries and soups.
How do you choose which foods to try? I usually look for popular street foods, regional specialties unique to that area, or just by wandering around to look for restaurants.
What’s a good strategy for trying foods in a new country? Start with small portions, ask locals for recommendations, and be open to unfamiliar ingredients or textures.
What’s a dish you’d love to try someday? One dish I’d love to try someday is spicy rice cakes or fried chicken from a street stand in Korea.
What is a must try street food? Vietnamese Banh mi, which is a refreshing sandwich packed with pickled vegetables and meats.
What dishes from other cultures have you made?

I enjoy making Korean food because of the simple ingredients and preparation. Some dishes I've made are kimbap, bibimbap, and Korean cold noodles.
How do you approach finding authentic foods abroad? I usually find authentic foods by exploring local markets or looking for street vendors.
What’s a good way to document your culinary adventures? I often take photos of the dishes and the restaurants to keep a food journal, where I also rate them and describe the flavors. It's a great way to remember experiences, track favorite dishes, and even recreate recipes at home.
What’s a food you didn’t like at first but learned to love? Egg tarts, I highly recommend them if you see them in a bakery
What’s a food adventure you’d like to try someday? Sampling street food in Korea, visiting a hawker center in Singapore, or exploring night markets in Thailand to experience the full diversity of flavors.
